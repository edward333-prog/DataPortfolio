Relevant Columns for Habitability Analysis:

Planetary Properties:

koi_prad (Planetary Radius): The size of the planet is crucial for habitability. Planets that are too large are likely to be gas giants, while those too small might not retain an atmosphere. Generally, Earth-like planets have a radius between 0.5 and 1.5 Earth radii.

koi_teq (Equilibrium Temperature): This tells us the surface temperature, which is important for liquid water to exist. A habitable planet typically has a temperature between 273 K and 373 K.

koi_insol (Insolation Flux): The amount of energy the planet receives from its star, which is key to maintaining conditions for life (e.g., liquid water). Suitable flux values often range between 0.35 and 1.5 Earth fluxes.

koi_period (Orbital Period): The length of the planet's orbit around the star, related to the planet’s distance from the star. This can help infer if the planet is in the habitable zone.

koi_depth (Transit Depth): Indicates how much of the star’s light is blocked by the planet during a transit, giving hints about the planet's size and atmosphere.

koi_duration (Transit Duration): Useful for understanding the orbital mechanics and how it relates to the planet's habitability.

Stellar Properties:
koi_steff (Stellar Effective Temperature): The temperature of the host star helps define the habitable zone around it. Cooler stars have closer habitable zones, and hotter stars have wider, more distant zones.

koi_slogg (Stellar Surface Gravity) and koi_srad (Stellar Radius): These help further characterize the star, which is important for calculating the boundaries of the habitable zone.

False Positive Indicators:
koi_fpflag_nt, koi_fpflag_ss, koi_fpflag_co, koi_fpflag_ec: These flags help filter out false positives, which are important to ensure that the candidate planets are genuine.

Position Data:
ra and dec (Right Ascension and Declination): The planet's position in the sky, useful for cross-referencing with other datasets or observations.
